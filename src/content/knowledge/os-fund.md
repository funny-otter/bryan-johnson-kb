# OS Fund

OS Fund is [bryan johnson](/knowledge/bryan-johnson/)'s deep-tech investment vehicle, created after the [braintree](/knowledge/braintree/) exit. Johnson's site describes it as funding scientist entrepreneurs who engineer atoms, molecules, and organisms across health, agriculture, manufacturing, and environmental care.

## Thesis

Chicago Booth reports Johnson established OS Fund with $100M of his own capital to back deep-tech scientist-entrepreneurs "rewriting the operating systems of life." The fund reflects his post-Braintree question: "How can I help humanity thrive?"

## Relationship to later work

OS Fund is the investment/infrastructure predecessor to [kernel](/knowledge/kernel/), [blueprint](/knowledge/blueprint/), and [dont die](/knowledge/dont-die/). Its language of "operating systems of life" anticipates Blueprint's body-as-system framing and Don't Die's civilization-as-survival-system framing.
